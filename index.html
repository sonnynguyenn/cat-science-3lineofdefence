LINES OF DEFENCE

First line of defence

Prevention is better than cure
First line of defence acts like a barrier to prevent pathogens from entering body instead of directly eliminate them. Analogy to gate guards preventing intruders (without permission) from entering a private building.

Second and third line of defence

Our lovely safe guards
Second and third line of defence work together as a security system. They alert the danger and gather guards to fight the trespasser.

Alerting

Some observable sympton when you get infected could be fever or swelling, this is the signal that your immune system or second line and third line of defence is protecting your body.

Fever

Fever is the increasing of body temperature above 38Â°C. The body is benefited as high temperature could slow down or kill some pathogens, this help the immune system in dealing with the pathogens.

Inflammation

Inflammation is redness and swelling at the cut or infected location. The swelling is formed as the damaged cells release chemicals that make fluid leak into near tissues in order to attract phagocytes.

Fighting

Leukocytes

Safe guards inside our bodies are leukocytes, a type of cell originated in bone marrow that responsible for fighting infection. These cells migrate from their bone marrow to bloodstream and lympathic system. Leukocytes are divided into two types, phagocytes and lymphocytes.

Phagocytes

Phagocytes send macrophages into the blood, they will consume any foregin cells they encounter. The consumed cells will be surrounded with membrance and form a vesicle, the macrophages release special enzymes to break down and neutralize the foreign cells. The phagocytes idenfity the antigen of the invaders and send the information to lymphocytes.
Source: Phagocytosis Animation - The Partnership In Education

Lymphocytes

A group of lymphocyte cells is T-cells search for infected body cells and kill them. While that, B-cells used information from phagocytes to create antibodies, which can destroy invading cells by matching the antigens like a latch. These antibodies are created and circulated through the body.
